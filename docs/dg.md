Differential Geometry
=====================

Here, we provide a minimal introduction to differential geometry from
physicists' perspective.
That is, we start with concepts that have obvious physics counterparts
such as particles and fields.


Physical and Geometric Objects
------------------------------

In classical physics, we work with a fundamental assumption there is
an objective reality independent of how it is described.
(One could argue, even in quantum mechanism, the wave functions are
such "objective reality".)

In order to work with these objective reality, we introduce the
concept of geometric objects which exist independent of coordinate
systems.

The simplest geometric object is just a point $p$, which is an element
of topological space $M$.
