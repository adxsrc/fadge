Differential Geometry
=====================

Here, we provide a minimal introduction to differential geometry.
While introduction to differential geometry often starts with
definition of manifolds, we realize that for physicists, it is much
more natural to first introduce concepts that have obvious physics
counterparts, namely particle and fields.


Physical and Geometric Objects
------------------------------

In physics, we assume there is an objective reality independent of it
is described.
(Even in quantum mechanism, the wave functions are the objective
reality.)
In order to work with these objective reality, we introduce the
concept of geometric objects which exist independent of coordinate
systems.

The simplest geometric object is just a point $p$, which is an element
of topological space $M$.
